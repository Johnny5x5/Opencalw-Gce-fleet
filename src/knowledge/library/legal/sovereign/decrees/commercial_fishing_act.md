# Title: The Sovereign Fishing & Aquatic Resource Act

## Section 1: Definition of Terms
1.  **Commercial Fishing:** The harvesting of fish, shellfish, or other aquatic life for the purpose of trade, sale, or profit.
2.  **Personal Fishing:** The harvesting of aquatic life for personal consumption or sustenance, not intended for sale.
3.  **Jubilee Year:** Every seventh (7th) year in the Sovereign Calendar.
4.  **Grand Jubilee Year:** Every fiftieth (50th) year in the Sovereign Calendar.
5.  **Maintenance Period:** The designated time during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year where commercial fishing operations cease for ecosystem recovery and industry maintenance.

## Section 2: The Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Jubilee Year (Year 7, 14, 21, etc.), all **Commercial Fishing** activities within the sovereign waters of the Republic are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Purpose:** This period is mandated to allow fish stocks to replenish and for the fishing industry to conduct maintenance on vessels and equipment without the pressure of harvest.
3.  **Duration:** The ban shall be in effect for the entire 12-month duration of the Jubilee Year.

## Section 3: The Grand Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Grand Jubilee Year (Year 50, 100, etc.), all **Commercial Fishing** activities are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Generational Renewal:** This period serves as a generational reset for the aquatic ecosystem, ensuring long-term sustainability for future citizens.

## Section 4: Personal Fishing Rights
1.  **Exemption:** **Personal Fishing** is explicitly **EXEMPT** from the Jubilee and Grand Jubilee moratoriums.
2.  **Right to Sustenance:** Citizens retain the right to fish for personal and family sustenance at all times, including during Jubilee years.

## Section 5: Enforcement & Penalties
1.  **Violation:** Engaging in Commercial Fishing during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year is a violation of the Sovereign Covenant.
2.  **Penalty:**
    *   **First Offense:** Confiscation of catch and a fine equal to 2x the market value of the catch.
    *   **Second Offense:** Confiscation of vessel and equipment.
    *   **Restitution:** All fines are paid into the **Ecological Restoration Trust**.
