# Title: The Sovereign Wildlife Management & Conservation Act

## Section 1: Definition of Terms
1.  **Commercial Hunting/Trapping:** The harvesting of wildlife for the purpose of trade, sale of furs/meat, or professional guiding services for profit.
2.  **Personal Hunting/Trapping:** The harvesting of wildlife for personal consumption (meat) or use (fur/hides).
3.  **Jubilee Year:** Every seventh (7th) year in the Sovereign Calendar, designated as a year of rest for wildlife.
4.  **Grand Jubilee Year:** Every fiftieth (50th) year in the Sovereign Calendar.
5.  **Protected Period:** The designated breeding season where no hunting is allowed, regardless of the year.

## Section 2: The Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Jubilee Year (Year 7, 14, 21, etc.), all **Commercial Hunting** and **Commercial Trapping** activities are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Guide Service Ban:** Professional hunting guide services are suspended during Jubilee years.
3.  **Purpose:** This period is mandated to allow animal populations to breed without commercial pressure and to ensure genetic diversity.

## Section 3: The Grand Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Grand Jubilee Year (Year 50, 100, etc.), all **Commercial Hunting** and **Trapping** activities are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Population Census:** A comprehensive wildlife census is conducted during this year to assess the health of all managed species.

## Section 4: Personal Hunting Rights
1.  **Exemption:** **Personal Hunting** for sustenance is **PERMITTED** during Jubilee and Grand Jubilee years, subject to standard bag limits.
2.  **Subsistence Priority:** Personal hunters have priority over commercial interests in all allocation decisions.
3.  **No Waste:** It is unlawful to kill an animal and leave the meat to waste. Wanton Waste is a crime against Purity (Moral Weight: 800).

## Section 5: Enforcement & Penalties
1.  **Violation:** Engaging in Commercial Hunting during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year is a violation of the Sovereign Covenant.
2.  **Penalty:**
    *   **First Offense:** Confiscation of all equipment (firearms, traps, vehicles) and a fine equal to 5x the market value of the animal.
    *   **Second Offense:** Permanent revocation of hunting privileges.
    *   **Restitution:** All fines are paid into the **Wildlife Conservation Trust**.
