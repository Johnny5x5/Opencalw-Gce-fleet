# Title: The Sovereign Mineral & Subsurface Stewardship Act

## Section 1: Definition of Terms
1.  **Commercial Mining:** The extraction, drilling, quarrying, or processing of minerals, metals, or fossil fuels for the purpose of trade, sale, or profit.
2.  **Jubilee Year:** Every seventh (7th) year in the Sovereign Calendar, designated as a year of cessation for extraction.
3.  **Grand Jubilee Year:** Every fiftieth (50th) year in the Sovereign Calendar.
4.  **Reclamation Period:** The designated time during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year where mining operations cease for environmental repair and safety audits.

## Section 2: The Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Jubilee Year (Year 7, 14, 21, etc.), all **Commercial Mining** extraction activities (active digging/drilling) are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Safety & Reclamation:** This period must be used for:
    *   **Safety Audits:** Inspecting shafts, equipment, and protocols.
    *   **Land Reclamation:** Repairing scarred land, replanting vegetation, and managing tailings.
    *   **Equipment Maintenance:** Repairing or upgrading machinery.
3.  **No New Starts:** No new mines or quarries may be broken ground during a Jubilee year.

## Section 3: The Grand Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Grand Jubilee Year (Year 50, 100, etc.), all **Commercial Mining** extraction activities are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Generational Review:** A mandatory review of all mining permits and environmental impact assessments occurs during this year.

## Section 4: Personal Use Rights
1.  **Exemption:** **Personal Collection** of surface rocks, minerals, or fossils (rockhounding) is **PERMITTED** during Jubilee and Grand Jubilee years, provided no mechanical equipment is used.
2.  **Artisanal Limit:** Small-scale artisanal mining is **RESTRICTED** to surface collection only during rest years.

## Section 5: Enforcement & Penalties
1.  **Violation:** Engaging in Commercial Extraction during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year is a violation of the Sovereign Covenant.
2.  **Penalty:**
    *   **First Offense:** Immediate shutdown of the mine for an additional 7 years.
    *   **Second Offense:** Permanent revocation of mining rights and forfeiture of all assets to the State.
    *   **Restitution:** All fines are paid into the **Land Reclamation Trust**.
