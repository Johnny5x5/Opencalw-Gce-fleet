# Title: The Sovereign Forestry & Timber Stewardship Act

## Section 1: Definition of Terms
1.  **Commercial Forestry:** The felling, skidding, processing, or transport of trees for the purpose of trade, sale, or profit.
2.  **Personal Use:** The gathering of dead, dying, or fallen timber for personal heating, cooking, or construction.
3.  **Jubilee Year:** Every seventh (7th) year in the Sovereign Calendar, designated as a year of rest for the forest.
4.  **Grand Jubilee Year:** Every fiftieth (50th) year in the Sovereign Calendar.
5.  **Regrowth Period:** The designated time during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year where commercial logging operations cease for forest regeneration.

## Section 2: The Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Jubilee Year (Year 7, 14, 21, etc.), all **Commercial Forestry** activities (felling of live trees) are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Purpose:** This period is mandated to allow saplings to establish without disturbance and for the forest ecosystem to recover from machinery impact.

## Section 3: The Grand Jubilee Moratorium
1.  **Commercial Ban:** During a Grand Jubilee Year (Year 50, 100, etc.), all **Commercial Forestry** activities are strictly prohibited.
2.  **Old Growth Preservation:** This period ensures that a percentage of the forest reaches maturity, providing vital habitat and carbon sequestration.

## Section 4: Personal Use Rights
1.  **Exemption:** **Personal Use** gathering of deadwood or fallen timber is explicitly **PERMITTED** during the Jubilee and Grand Jubilee years.
2.  **Live Tree Restriction:** The felling of live trees for personal use is **RESTRICTED** during rest years unless necessary for emergency shelter or safety.

## Section 5: Enforcement & Penalties
1.  **Violation:** Engaging in Commercial Forestry during a Jubilee or Grand Jubilee year is a violation of the Sovereign Covenant.
2.  **Penalty:**
    *   **First Offense:** Confiscation of timber and equipment used, plus a fine equal to 3x the market value of the timber.
    *   **Second Offense:** Permanent revocation of logging rights.
    *   **Restitution:** All fines are paid into the **Reforestation Trust**.
